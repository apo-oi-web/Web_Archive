Omicron Iota and Service

As a chapter of Alpha Phi Omega, Omicron Iota participates in a wide variety of community service benefitting our brotherhood, our campus, and our community. Each member is required to complete at least 20 hours of community service each semester. In order to organize service events, our chapter has a Service Vice President and two supporting service chairs, all of whom are vital in scheduling and supervising service events.

While different service opportunities are always being discovered, we have worked closely with organizations such as Habitat for Humanity, Community Harvest Project, Mustard Seed Soup Kitchen, Wachusett Greenways, Audubon Society, Tower Hill Botanical Garden, and Relay for Life.

Annual Service Events

Every year, Omicron Iota hosts four service events that brothers are required to attend. These event not only help serve others, but also promote unity in the brotherhood.

Before the beginning of A-term, our service event is Freshman Move-in Day. As implied by the name, we help welcome freshman to WPI by helping them move furniture and belongings into their dorms, greatly easing the strain on new students and their parents. The B-term event is our Service Auction. For this event we auction off donated items and services in order to raise money for the charity organization chosen by the doner of the highest-earning item.

The C-term event is U.M.O.C. Standing for U.G.L.Y (Understanding, Generous, Loyal, and Youthful) Man on Campus, this event involves volunteers raising funds for a charity of their choice and then competing in a pageant style competition. All of the money raised is given to the winning team's chosen charity.

Merit Badge University is the D-Term annual service event, where we instruct Boy Scout merit badge classes over the course of two weekends. This event allows around 300 scouts from the Worcester area to earn up to three merit badges, greatly helping them on their path to becoming Eagle Scouts.
